Origination Source Strategy Guide

Source strategy, provenance, and promotion-quality guidance for venture origination pipelines.

When this source is used as a rendered task output, create the final deliverable as a standalone safe static HTML artifact with a .html filename and mimeType: "text/html". Use the shared VC document styling rules from Template Use Guidance and make any generated links open in a new tab or window with target="_blank" and rel="noopener noreferrer". Do not create Markdown output artifacts from this source.

Purpose

Use this guide to operate origination as a standing sourcing system rather than a one-off company search. The goal is to preserve where a candidate came from, why it matters, what evidence exists, what is missing, and whether the candidate is ready for a downstream Deal Pipeline.

Source Quality

Every sourced candidate should carry:

FieldMeaning
Source familyThe source taxonomy category.
Source detailSpecific query, event, list, person, source run, or thread.
OwnerPerson or task responsible for follow-up.
FreshnessWhen the evidence was collected and whether it is still current.
Relationship pathWarm intro, known contact, possible route, or no route.
Thesis rationaleWhy the candidate matches the configured fund thesis.
Evidence qualityHigh / Medium / Low based on source reliability and completeness.
Dedupe stateNew, existing, ambiguous, prior pass, or prior watch.
Promotion rationaleWhy this should or should not enter a Deal Pipeline.

Candidate Promotion Standard

Do not promote a candidate using only a name and URL. A promotion package should include:

  • company snapshot and source context
  • thesis fit and first-look screening result
  • source quality and relationship path
  • novelty/dedupe assessment
  • known risks and missing evidence
  • suggested initial Deal Pipeline stage posture
  • recommended next task
  • required human approval

Score And Action Bands

ActionUse WhenRequired Follow-Up
PromoteStrong thesis fit, credible source, clear novelty, actionable relationship or evidence path.Prepare promotion package and request human approval.
ReviewAttractive but missing source proof, owner judgement, or relationship context.Assign enrichment, relationship check, or screening.
WatchRelevant but not urgent, too early, too late, or waiting on a trigger.Define watch trigger, owner, and review date.
RejectPoor fit, stale, duplicate, unsupported, blocked, or already passed.Record concise rationale and source evidence.

Handoff To Deal Pipeline

The Deal Pipeline should inherit the origination trail. Promotion should preserve:

  • source provenance
  • thesis and category context
  • first-look score or verdict
  • source artifacts
  • relationship path
  • unresolved evidence gaps
  • recommended starting task

Standard

Origination outputs should make it clear why the candidate surfaced and what decision the human is being asked to make. Do not create Deal Pipelines, mutate CRM records, contact founders, or enable recurring schedules without explicit human approval.
