# Formal Diligence Workstream Guide

## Purpose

Use this guide when an opportunity has moved beyond initial evaluation and the investor needs structured verification. Formal diligence should test the assumptions that decide the investment, not summarize the company narrative.

Each workstream should produce findings, confidence, evidence, open questions, decision impact, and escalation notes.

## Workstream Map

| Workstream | Information Requests | Key Outputs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Leadership and team | Founder CVs, role map, references, board/adviser details, hiring plan. | Founder/team assessment, reference findings, key-hire gaps, governance needs. |
| Product, technical, and IP | Product description, architecture, roadmap, patents, data dependencies, open-source use, security context. | Technical risk, IP defensibility, scalability, roadmap realism, expert questions. |
| Customer and GTM | Customer/prospect list, pipeline, pricing, sales cycle, pilots, churn/retention, partner references. | Customer validation, buyer urgency, pipeline quality, pricing power, need-to-have assessment. |
| Market and competition | Segmented market estimates, competitor list, alternatives, market-share assumptions, category reports. | TAM/SAM/SOM assessment, competitive map, incumbent/substitution risk, market-development risk. |
| Financial | P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, forecast, bank statements, burn, runway, use of funds, financing plan. | Forecast realism, unit economics, runway, sensitivity, financing risk, milestone sufficiency. |
| Legal, regulatory, and employment | Corporate docs, shareholder docs, contracts, employment, IP assignments, litigation, licenses, regulatory strategy. | Legal document index, issue register, counsel questions, showstopper-risk review support. |
| Company structure and deal terms | Incorporation records, cap table, option pool, shareholder rights, proposed terms, co-investor context. | Structural issues, ownership path, terms questions, closing-readiness blockers. |
| Exit and return fit | Comparable exits, likely acquirers, IPO logic, strategic buyer rationale, return model assumptions. | Exit logic, valuation basis, fund-return fit, downside and upside sensitivity. |

## Workstream Output Standard

| Section | Required Content |
| --- | --- |
| Scope | What the workstream covers and excludes. |
| Source inputs | Source artifacts, interviews, models, third-party evidence, counsel notes, and source quality. |
| Verified facts | Claims that are supported by reliable evidence. |
| Unverified claims | Company or third-party assertions that still need proof. |
| Findings | Decision-relevant positives, negatives, and neutral context. |
| Confidence | High / Medium / Low by finding, not only by report. |
| Risks | Severity, likelihood, mitigant, owner, and decision impact. |
| Must-answer questions | Questions that can change pass/proceed, valuation, terms, or approval conditions. |
| Escalation triggers | Issues that require partner, IC, counsel, expert, or founder follow-up. |

## Diligence Question Generation

Questions should be generated from known evidence gaps. For each question, include:

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Workstream | Commercial, financial, technical, founder/team, legal, market, terms, or exit. |
| Question | Specific, answerable question. |
| Evidence requested | Artifact, interview, model, contract, source link, export, or expert input. |
| Why it matters | The decision, risk, score, or term it affects. |
| Owner | Human or workstream owner. |
| Priority | Must answer / Should answer / Optional. |
| Success threshold | What would clear the question or materially improve confidence. |

## Escalation Rules

Escalate when:

- a must-answer question remains unanswered before decision review or term-sheet work
- a legal, regulatory, IP, or ownership issue could block investment
- the company narrative contradicts customer, financial, technical, or public evidence
- forecast assumptions do not support the proposed financing plan
- the valuation or ownership path is inconsistent with fund-return requirements
- a workstream uncovers a severe risk with no credible mitigant

## Completion Standard

Formal diligence is complete only when must-answer questions have an answer, owner, or explicit approval condition. Do not imply counsel approval, legal sufficiency, external communication, stage movement, or administrative execution unless a human has separately approved and the platform action exists.
