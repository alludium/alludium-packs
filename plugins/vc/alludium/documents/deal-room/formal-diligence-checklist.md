# Formal Diligence Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist during formal diligence. It combines the major diligence workstreams into an assignable verification plan. Each item should have a status, source evidence, owner, confidence, and decision impact.

## Checklist Format

| Workstream | Item | Evidence Requested | Task | Key Question | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |  |  | Open / In review / Verified / Blocked / Not applicable |

## Leadership And Team

| Item | Evidence Requested | Key Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CEO and leadership capability | Resumes, role descriptions, founder history, references. | Does the CEO have the experience, judgement, and leadership ability to succeed? |
| Team completeness | Org chart, hiring plan, role coverage. | Does the team cover the critical functions required for the next stage? |
| References | Professional references, customer/partner references where relevant. | Do references confirm capability, integrity, and execution quality? |
| Board and advisers | Board/adviser list, bios, time commitment, role. | Are board members and advisers suitable, committed, and useful? |
| Key hires | Hiring roadmap and current gaps. | What hires are needed to address execution risks? |

## Technical, Product, And IP

| Item | Evidence Requested | Key Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Technology and product description | Product overview, demo, architecture notes. | What has been built, and does it work? |
| Architecture and scalability | Architecture diagram, infrastructure notes, integration plan. | Can the solution scale without unacceptable technical or services burden? |
| Technical team | Technical team bios, responsibilities, hiring plan. | Is the technical team qualified and experienced? |
| IP position | Patent filings, invention assignments, IP ownership records, licensing agreements. | How strong and clean are the IP positions? |
| Roadmap | Product roadmap, milestones, dependencies. | Is the roadmap achievable with the team and funding plan? |
| Competing technologies | Competitor and substitute technology review. | Are superior technologies likely to emerge or already available? |

## Customer, GTM, And Commercial

| Item | Evidence Requested | Key Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer need | Customer calls, references, pilots, contracts, usage. | Do customers confirm need, urgency, and likely adoption? |
| GTM plan | Sales approach, buyer roles, partner strategy, milestones. | Is the go-to-market plan credible and repeatable? |
| Pipeline | Sales pipeline by stage, probability, owner, and next action. | Is the pipeline adequate and realistically weighted? |
| Pricing and revenue | Pricing materials, contracts, LOIs, revenue model. | Are pricing and revenue assumptions credible? |
| Customer experience | Customer feedback, support notes, churn/retention evidence. | What concerns or improvements do customers highlight? |
| Commercial risk | Adoption, awareness, sales-cycle, concentration, or channel risks. | What could block commercial scale? |

## Market And Competition

| Item | Evidence Requested | Key Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Market estimates | Segmented market estimates, published sources, bottom-up logic. | Are top-down and bottom-up estimates consistent and attractive? |
| Market share assumptions | Forecast share assumptions, comparable adoption paths. | Are market-share projections realistic? |
| Competitive map | Competitors, substitutes, relative strengths and weaknesses. | Is the company well positioned against current and future competitors? |
| Barriers and defensibility | IP, data, network effects, switching costs, distribution. | What makes the position defensible? |
| Expert input | Expert interviews or industry references where needed. | What do independent sources confirm or dispute? |

## Financial

| Item | Evidence Requested | Key Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Historical financials | P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, bank statements where available. | Do historicals and cash evidence make sense? |
| Forecast model | Three-year forecast, assumptions, sensitivities. | Are projections reasonable and conservative? |
| Burn and runway | Current cash, burn, runway, hiring plan. | Can the company survive to the next funding milestone? |
| Use of funds | Past and projected use of funds. | Are proceeds sufficient and appropriately allocated? |
| Future financing | Future capital needs and assumptions. | Is future financing risk manageable? |
| Ownership and valuation | Cap table, option pool, valuation, round structure. | Are ownership and valuation compatible with fund-return goals? |

## Legal, Regulatory, And Company Structure

| Item | Evidence Requested | Key Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Entity and filings | Incorporation documents, statutory filings, registers. | Is the company validly formed and current? |
| Corporate approvals | Board/shareholder approvals, consent rights. | Are there approval rights or blockers? |
| Litigation | Litigation searches, disputes, claims. | Is there any material litigation or unresolved dispute? |
| IP legal status | Assignments, licenses, patents, copyrights, trade secrets. | Does the company own or control the IP it depends on? |
| Employment and consultants | Employment agreements, consultant agreements, invention assignment. | Are contributor rights and obligations documented? |
| Regulatory | Regulatory strategy, licenses, correspondence. | Is the regulatory path feasible and funded? |
| Deal terms | Proposed terms, investor rights, control rights, economics. | What terms should be changed, negotiated, or escalated? |

## Exit And Return Fit

| Item | Evidence Requested | Key Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Exit strategy | Exit thesis, likely acquirers, comparable outcomes. | Is the exit strategy reasonable and aligned with the team? |
| Exit multiples | Comparable valuations and scenario model. | What exit multiples can be predicted under representative scenarios? |
| Buyer relationships | Founder network, strategic relationship map. | Can the company create the relationships needed for exit interest? |

## Completion Standard

Each must-answer item should be verified, blocked with owner/action, waived by explicit human decision, or converted into an approval condition. Do not mark formal diligence complete when source artifacts are missing or unresolved risks remain unowned.

## Boundary

This checklist supports investor diligence coordination. It does not provide legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, technical-security, or investment advice. Specialist conclusions should be attributed to the relevant human reviewer, counsel, accountant, expert, or approved source artifact.
