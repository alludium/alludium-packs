Commercial Evaluation Guide

Lightweight commercial evaluation guidance for market, customer, GTM, pricing, and competition hypotheses.

When this source is used as a rendered task output, create the final deliverable as a standalone safe static HTML artifact with a .html filename and mimeType: "text/html". Use the shared VC document styling rules from Template Use Guidance and make any generated links open in a new tab or window with target="_blank" and rel="noopener noreferrer". Do not create Markdown output artifacts from this source.

Purpose

Use this guide to evaluate commercial attractiveness before formal diligence. The output should identify whether there is a credible customer segment, urgent pain, budget, business model, and path to adoption.

Core Questions

AreaEvaluation QuestionEvidence To Seek
Customer segmentWho is the first narrow customer segment?Deck, website, call notes, customer examples, pipeline snippets.
Pain and urgencyIs the problem painful enough to buy now?Customer quotes, workflow evidence, budget owner, usage or pilot signals.
Budget and pricingWho pays, how much, and from what budget?Pricing notes, contracts, LOIs, benchmarks, buyer role.
GTM pathHow does the company reach customers repeatably?Sales motion, channels, partner routes, founder network, pipeline.
CompetitionWhat alternatives exist and why can this company win?Competitor list, incumbent/substitute analysis, customer switching logic.
Market sizeIs the first segment and broader category large enough?Segmented market estimates, bottom-up logic, public data.
Commercial riskWhat assumption can most quickly break the case?Evidence gaps, contradictory signals, weak adoption proof.

Output Standard

SectionRequired Content
Current commercial viewPositive / mixed / negative / unknown.
Main commercial thesisThe customer or market belief that must be true.
Evidence and confidenceSource-backed evidence with High / Medium / Low confidence.
Open proof gapsWhat customer, pipeline, pricing, or market evidence is missing.
Next questionsFounder, customer, partner, expert, or data-room questions.
Decision impactWhether the opportunity should pass, watch, continue evaluation, or enter targeted diligence.

Boundary

Commercial evaluation is not customer validation diligence. Do not state that demand is verified unless customer references, contracts, usage, or equivalent direct evidence have been reviewed.
