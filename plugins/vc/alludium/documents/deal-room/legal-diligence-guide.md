# Legal Diligence Guide

## Purpose

Use this guide during formal diligence to organize legal source documents, identify factual gaps, draft counsel questions, and track legal-risk issues for human and counsel review. The agent must not provide legal advice or approve legal sufficiency.

## Core Workstreams

| Workstream | Evidence Requested | Key Questions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corporate existence and filings | Incorporation documents, constitutional documents, registers, filings. | Is the company validly formed, current, and in good standing? |
| Capitalization and ownership | Cap table, option pool, shareholder register, prior financing documents. | Are there ownership, option, debt, or undocumented issuance issues? |
| Corporate approvals | Board minutes, shareholder consents, investor rights, approval rights. | Who must approve the transaction, and are there blockers? |
| IP ownership and licensing | Patents, copyrights, trade secrets, assignments, licenses, contributor agreements. | Does the company own or control the IP and data it depends on? |
| Employment and consultants | Employment contracts, consultant contracts, invention assignment, restrictive covenants. | Are contributor rights and obligations documented? |
| Material contracts | Customer, supplier, partner, distribution, data, and license agreements. | Do contracts create rights, restrictions, concentration, termination, or change-of-control risks? |
| Litigation and disputes | Litigation searches, threatened claims, settlement agreements, correspondence. | Are there disputes that could materially affect the investment? |
| Regulatory and compliance | Licenses, regulatory strategy, privacy/security materials, correspondence. | Are regulatory obligations understood and feasible? |
| Transaction terms | Proposed security, investor rights, control rights, liquidation/exit rights, covenants. | Which provisions require counsel, partner, or IC attention? |

## Counsel Question Standard

Good counsel questions are specific and tied to a source document or missing artifact. For each question, state:

- source document or missing evidence
- factual observation
- why it matters to the transaction
- requested counsel review or confirmation
- deadline or dependency

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately when there are indications of:

- unclear ownership of material IP, data, or product assets
- undocumented share issuances, options, SAFEs, loans, or side letters
- unresolved litigation, threatened claims, or regulatory exposure
- missing founder, employee, consultant, or contractor IP assignments
- blocking investor approval rights, consent rights, or unusual control terms
- material contract restrictions, termination rights, or change-of-control issues
- transaction terms inconsistent with approved commercial terms

## Boundary

The agent may organize, summarize, cross-reference, and draft questions. The agent must not interpret legal sufficiency, advise on enforceability, clear a risk, negotiate legal terms, contact counsel, or contact the company without explicit human approval and platform-supported action.
