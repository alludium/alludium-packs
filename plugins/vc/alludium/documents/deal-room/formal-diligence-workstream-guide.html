Formal Diligence Workstream Guide

Workstream guide for verification-oriented venture diligence.

When this source is used as a rendered task output, create the final deliverable as a standalone safe static HTML artifact with a .html filename and mimeType: "text/html". Use the shared VC document styling rules from Template Use Guidance and make any generated links open in a new tab or window with target="_blank" and rel="noopener noreferrer". Do not create Markdown output artifacts from this source.

Purpose

Use this guide when an opportunity has moved beyond initial evaluation and the investor needs structured verification. Formal diligence should test the assumptions that decide the investment, not summarize the company narrative.

Each workstream should produce findings, confidence, evidence, open questions, decision impact, and escalation notes.

ScopeWhat the workstream covers and excludes.

Unverified claimsCompany or third-party assertions that still need proof.
FindingsDecision-relevant positives, negatives, and neutral context.
ConfidenceHigh / Medium / Low by finding, not only by report.

Diligence Question Generation

Questions should be generated from known evidence gaps. For each question, include:

FieldMeaning
WorkstreamCommercial, financial, technical, founder/team, legal, market, terms, or exit.
QuestionSpecific, answerable question.
Evidence requestedArtifact, interview, model, contract, source link, export, or expert input.
Why it mattersThe decision, risk, score, or term it affects.
OwnerHuman or workstream owner.
PriorityMust answer / Should answer / Optional.
Success thresholdWhat would clear the question or materially improve confidence.

Escalation Rules

Escalate when:

  • a must-answer question remains unanswered before decision review or term-sheet work
  • a legal, regulatory, IP, or ownership issue could block investment
  • the company narrative contradicts customer, financial, technical, or public evidence
  • forecast assumptions do not support the proposed financing plan
  • the valuation or ownership path is inconsistent with fund-return requirements
  • a workstream uncovers a severe risk with no credible mitigant

Completion Standard

Formal diligence is complete only when must-answer questions have an answer, owner, or explicit approval condition. Do not imply counsel approval, legal sufficiency, external communication, stage movement, or administrative execution unless a human has separately approved and the platform action exists.
