Formal Diligence Checklist

Full formal diligence checklist covering leadership, technical, commercial, market, financial, legal, deal-terms, exit, and company-structure workstreams.

When this source is used as a rendered task output, create the final deliverable as a standalone safe static HTML artifact with a .html filename and mimeType: "text/html". Use the shared VC document styling rules from Template Use Guidance and make any generated links open in a new tab or window with target="_blank" and rel="noopener noreferrer". Do not create Markdown output artifacts from this source.

Purpose

Use this checklist during formal diligence. It combines the major diligence workstreams into an assignable verification plan. Each item should have a status, source evidence, owner, confidence, and decision impact.

Checklist Format

WorkstreamItemEvidence RequestedTaskKey QuestionStatus
Open / In review / Verified / Blocked / Not applicable

Leadership And Team

ItemEvidence RequestedKey Question
CEO and leadership capabilityResumes, role descriptions, founder history, references.Does the CEO have the experience, judgement, and leadership ability to succeed?
Team completenessOrg chart, hiring plan, role coverage.Does the team cover the critical functions required for the next stage?
ReferencesProfessional references, customer/partner references where relevant.Do references confirm capability, integrity, and execution quality?
Board and advisersBoard/adviser list, bios, time commitment, role.Are board members and advisers suitable, committed, and useful?
Key hiresHiring roadmap and current gaps.What hires are needed to address execution risks?

Technical, Product, And IP

ItemEvidence RequestedKey Question
Technology and product descriptionProduct overview, demo, architecture notes.What has been built, and does it work?
Architecture and scalabilityArchitecture diagram, infrastructure notes, integration plan.Can the solution scale without unacceptable technical or services burden?
Technical teamTechnical team bios, responsibilities, hiring plan.Is the technical team qualified and experienced?
IP positionPatent filings, invention assignments, IP ownership records, licensing agreements.How strong and clean are the IP positions?
RoadmapProduct roadmap, milestones, dependencies.Is the roadmap achievable with the team and funding plan?
Competing technologiesCompetitor and substitute technology review.Are superior technologies likely to emerge or already available?

Customer, GTM, And Commercial

ItemEvidence RequestedKey Question
Customer needCustomer calls, references, pilots, contracts, usage.Do customers confirm need, urgency, and likely adoption?
GTM planSales approach, buyer roles, partner strategy, milestones.Is the go-to-market plan credible and repeatable?
PipelineSales pipeline by stage, probability, owner, and next action.Is the pipeline adequate and realistically weighted?
Pricing and revenuePricing materials, contracts, LOIs, revenue model.Are pricing and revenue assumptions credible?
Customer experienceCustomer feedback, support notes, churn/retention evidence.What concerns or improvements do customers highlight?
Commercial riskAdoption, awareness, sales-cycle, concentration, or channel risks.What could block commercial scale?

Market And Competition

ItemEvidence RequestedKey Question
Market estimatesSegmented market estimates, published sources, bottom-up logic.Are top-down and bottom-up estimates consistent and attractive?
Market share assumptionsForecast share assumptions, comparable adoption paths.Are market-share projections realistic?
Competitive mapCompetitors, substitutes, relative strengths and weaknesses.Is the company well positioned against current and future competitors?
Barriers and defensibilityIP, data, network effects, switching costs, distribution.What makes the position defensible?
Expert inputExpert interviews or industry references where needed.What do independent sources confirm or dispute?

Financial

ItemEvidence RequestedKey Question
Historical financialsP&L, balance sheet, cash flow, bank statements where available.Do historicals and cash evidence make sense?
Forecast modelThree-year forecast, assumptions, sensitivities.Are projections reasonable and conservative?
Burn and runwayCurrent cash, burn, runway, hiring plan.Can the company survive to the next funding milestone?
Use of fundsPast and projected use of funds.Are proceeds sufficient and appropriately allocated?
Future financingFuture capital needs and assumptions.Is future financing risk manageable?
Ownership and valuationCap table, option pool, valuation, round structure.Are ownership and valuation compatible with fund-return goals?

Legal, Regulatory, And Company Structure

ItemEvidence RequestedKey Question
Entity and filingsIncorporation documents, statutory filings, registers.Is the company validly formed and current?
Corporate approvalsBoard/shareholder approvals, consent rights.Are there approval rights or blockers?
LitigationLitigation searches, disputes, claims.Is there any material litigation or unresolved dispute?
IP legal statusAssignments, licenses, patents, copyrights, trade secrets.Does the company own or control the IP it depends on?
Employment and consultantsEmployment agreements, consultant agreements, invention assignment.Are contributor rights and obligations documented?
RegulatoryRegulatory strategy, licenses, correspondence.Is the regulatory path feasible and funded?
Deal termsProposed terms, investor rights, control rights, economics.What terms should be changed, negotiated, or escalated?

Exit And Return Fit

ItemEvidence RequestedKey Question
Exit strategyExit thesis, likely acquirers, comparable outcomes.Is the exit strategy reasonable and aligned with the team?
Exit multiplesComparable valuations and scenario model.What exit multiples can be predicted under representative scenarios?
Buyer relationshipsFounder network, strategic relationship map.Can the company create the relationships needed for exit interest?

Completion Standard

Each must-answer item should be verified, blocked with owner/action, waived by explicit human decision, or converted into an approval condition. Do not mark formal diligence complete when source artifacts are missing or unresolved risks remain unowned.

Boundary

This checklist supports investor diligence coordination. It does not provide legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, technical-security, or investment advice. Specialist conclusions should be attributed to the relevant human reviewer, counsel, accountant, expert, or approved source artifact.
