# Opportunity Evaluation Framework

## Purpose

Use this framework after a first-look screen when the investor needs a deeper view of whether an opportunity should move to targeted diligence, decision review, term-sheet consideration, watchlist, or pass. It is not a completed diligence report. It is an evidence-led investment judgement artifact.

The output should explain the current posture, the central thesis tension, the strongest positive evidence, the must-prove assumptions, and the next evidence-gathering plan.

## Evaluation Lenses

| Lens | Investor Question | Evidence Standard |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Team | Can this team execute the critical success factors and hire around gaps? | Founder history, references, role coverage, motivation, integrity, hiring plan, board and adviser quality. |
| Micro market | Which first customer segment has urgent pain, budget, and willingness to pay now? | Customer interviews, pilots, pipeline, pricing evidence, use-case specificity, need-to-have proof. |
| Macro market | Is the broader category large and growing enough for venture outcomes? | Segment growth, spend pools, adoption drivers, market reports, macro tailwinds. |
| Macro industry | Are industry forces attractive, or will buyers, suppliers, incumbents, substitutes, or regulators capture value? | Industry structure, buyer power, data or rights control, rivalry, substitution, regulatory exposure. |
| Micro industry | What is the company's durable edge and why is it hard to copy? | IP, proprietary data, process advantage, distribution, switching costs, technical depth, strategic positioning. |
| Timing | Is now the right moment for adoption, infrastructure, regulation, and buyer behavior? | Technology readiness, budget cycles, regulation, platform shifts, customer behavior, inflection events. |
| Scalability | Can the business scale through product leverage rather than bespoke services? | Architecture, repeatability, gross margin, integration burden, operating model, product roadmap. |
| Next-round readiness | Can the company reach the proof level needed for the next financing within the expected runway? | ARR or equivalent milestone path, pipeline coverage, retention, usage, burn, runway, hiring plan. |
| Financial and ownership fit | Can the fund own enough at a reasonable valuation with manageable reserve and financing risk? | Cap table, round size, valuation, use of funds, follow-on needs, exit math. |
| Legal, regulatory, and IP | Are there legal, data, employment, IP, regulatory, or transaction issues that block investment? | Counsel inputs, corporate docs, IP assignments, litigation searches, licenses, compliance map. |

## Evaluation Tracker

The checklist layer exists to preserve coverage and discipline. It should not force a mechanical average.

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Area | Team, market, product, financial, legal, deal terms, exit, fund fit, or other relevant family. |
| Criterion | The specific question or criterion being assessed. |
| Score | Conservative evidence-led score. Do not reward unverified claims as proof. |
| Confidence | High / Medium / Low based on source quality and recency. |
| Evidence | Source artifact, interview, public source, model, data-room item, or counsel input. |
| Gap | Missing or unverified proof. |
| Decision impact | Pass, watch, meeting, targeted diligence, decision review, term-sheet readiness, or condition. |

## Posture Bands

| Score Band | Posture | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `<50` | Watchlist / do not progress | Too many unresolved proof gaps unless new evidence changes the case. |
| `50-59` | Thesis-clarification meeting | The team or category may justify a meeting, but the core investment thesis is unresolved. |
| `60-69` | Targeted diligence | Worth structured evidence collection, but not decision-review ready. |
| `70-79` | Decision-review preparation | Potential review candidate if the improvement is evidence-driven. |
| `80-84` | Term-sheet consideration | Possible draft term sheet if valuation, ownership, gating risks, and partner consensus fit. |
| `85+` | High-conviction candidate | Strong case subject to negotiation, formal diligence, and approval conditions. |

A high blended score must not override a gating submodel. Technical proof, legal/IP, customer proof, financial assumptions, team completeness, or fund ownership can each block progression.

## Report Standard

Opportunity evaluations should include:

1. Current recommendation and stage posture.
2. Best case, base case, and main gating risk.
3. Single most important thesis question.
4. What is attractive.
5. What must be proved.
6. Narrative lens assessment.
7. Score table with confidence and rationale.
8. Central thesis tension.
9. Workstream-specific analysis.
10. Meeting agenda and data-room request list.
11. Recommended next steps.
12. Evaluation tracker.
13. Source register and claim-hygiene notes.

## Evidence Rules

Separate company claims, verified evidence, investor inference, and missing proof. Use conservative scores when the evidence is founder-provided only, stale, uncited, or not independently checked. State what evidence would improve, reduce, or block the recommendation.

## Meeting And Data-Room Plan

For each gating risk, produce:

| Output | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Question | Specific question for founder, customer, expert, counsel, or internal team. |
| Evidence requested | Artifact, data-room item, model, transcript, contract, source link, or interview. |
| Why it matters | Which investment decision or risk it affects. |
| Owner | Human or workstream owner. |
| Timing | Before meeting, after meeting, before decision review, before term sheet, or before closing. |
| Success threshold | What proof would clear or reduce the risk. |

## Standard

End with a concrete recommendation. Do not hide behind a score. The evaluation should make clear whether the next best action is pass, watch, targeted diligence, partner review, decision-review preparation, or term-sheet consideration.
